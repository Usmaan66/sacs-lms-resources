# 2.1 Kingdom Monera
- Includes all prokaryotic organisms, mainly **bacteria**.  
- They are the most **abundant** and **diverse** micro-organisms.
---
## Habitat
- Found in **extreme conditions**:
  - Hot springs  
  - Deserts  
  - Snow  
  - Deep oceans  
---
## Nutrition
- Show high **metabolic diversity**:
  - **Autotrophic**: Make their own food  
    - *Photosynthetic*  
    - *Chemosynthetic*
  - **Heterotrophic**: Depend on others for food  
---
## Shapes of Bacteria
1. **Coccus** – Spherical  
   *Example: Streptococcus pneumoniae*
2. **Bacillus** – Rod-shaped  
   *Examples: E. coli, Bacillus anthracis*
3. **Spirillum** – Spiral  
   *Examples: Spirillum, Treponema*
4. **Vibrio** – Comma-shaped  
   *Example: Vibrio cholerae*
---
## Types of Bacteria
### 2.1.1 Archaebacteria
- Live in **harsh environments**:
  - Salty areas (*halophiles*)  
  - Hot springs (*thermoacidophiles*)  
  - Marshes (*methanogens*)  
- Have a **unique cell wall** to survive extreme conditions  
- **Methanogens** produce **biogas (methane)**
---
### 2.1.2 Eubacteria
- Have a **rigid cell wall**  
- May have **flagella** for movement

#### Cyanobacteria (Blue-green algae)
- Have **chlorophyll** like plants  
- Are **photosynthetic**  
- Can be:
  - *Unicellular*  
  - *Colonial*  
  - *Filamentous*  
- Found in:
  - Freshwater  
  - Marine  
  - Soil habitats  
- Some fix **nitrogen** using **heterocysts**  
  - *Examples: Nostoc, Anabaena*
---
## Nutrition in Eubacteria
- **Chemosynthetic autotrophs**: Use **inorganic substances** (e.g., nitrates, nitrites) for energy  
- **Heterotrophs**:
  - Act as **decomposers**  
  - Impact human life (e.g., **curd formation**, **antibiotics**)
---
## Reproduction in Bacteria
- **Binary fission** – In favorable conditions  
- **Spore formation** – In unfavorable conditions  
- **Primitive sexual reproduction** – DNA transfer between bacteria
---
## Mycoplasma
- Bacteria **without a cell wall**  
- **Smallest living cells**  
- Can survive **without oxygen**  
- Some are **pathogenic** in plants and animals
